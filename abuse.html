Abuse Reporting and Acceptable Use Policy

We are committed to providing a safe, respectful, and functional environment for all users. This policy outlines what constitutes abuse and the proper procedure for reporting it.

1. What Constitutes Abuse (Good Form for Abuse)

Abuse includes, but is not limited to, any conduct that violates local, state, federal, or international laws, or interferes with the normal operation of our services. Examples of abuse include:

  • Spamming: Unsolicited bulk email, repetitive or irrelevant posts, or distributing unsolicited commercial communication.
  • Illegal Content: Posting, sharing, or linking to child exploitation, terrorist material, or other content deemed illegal.
  • Harassment and Hate Speech: Targeted harassment, threats, or the promotion of discrimination, hatred, or violence against individuals or groups.
  • Intellectual Property Infringement: Unauthorized use of copyrighted material, trademarks, or trade secrets.
  • Malicious Activity: Distributing malware, phishing, or attempting to compromise the security or integrity of our systems or other users' data.

2. Reporting Procedure

If you encounter content or activity that violates this policy, please follow the formal reporting procedure below. Providing detailed information allows us to investigate and resolve the issue quickly.

Please submit all abuse reports via email to the following dedicated address:

abuse@int.yt

Your report must include:

  • Your contact information (name and email).
  • A clear description of the alleged abuse.
  • The exact location (URL, username, or IP address) where the abuse occurred.
  • Any supporting evidence, such as screenshots or communication logs.

3. Policy Violations and Consequences

We take all reports seriously and will investigate them promptly and confidentially. Violations of this policy may result in immediate action, including:

  • Removal of the offending content.
  • Temporary or permanent suspension of the user's account.
  • Termination of service.
  • Reporting the activity to appropriate law enforcement agencies.
